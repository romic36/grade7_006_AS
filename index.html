The Awesome "How-To" Guide

Objective

To practice creating clear and organized lists using both bullet points and numbers.

Instructions

  1. Students will be given two topics: "Things to Bring on a Beach Outing" and "Steps for Playing Piko" (Hopscotch).
  2. For "Things to Bring on a Beach Outing," students will create a list using bullet points. This is for items that don't need a specific order. They should list at least five items.
  3. For "Steps for Playing Piko," students will create a list using numbers. This is for actions that must be done in a specific sequence. They should list at least five steps.
  4. After completing the lists, they will be asked to explain the difference between when to use bullet points versus when to use numbers.

Possible Listings

Here are 10 possible listings, with 5 for each type of list, that students could come up with. These examples are rooted in common Filipino experiences and daily life.

Numbered List Examples

  1. Steps for Playing Piko:
    • Draw the hopscotch pattern on the pavement.
    • Throw the marker (pamato) into the first box.
    • Hop to the last box without stepping on the box with the marker.
    • On the way back, pick up the marker.
    • Continue the process until you have reached the last box with the marker.
  2. How to Make a Paper Parol:
    • Take two square sheets of paper and fold them in half to create triangles.
    • Overlap the two triangles to form a star.
    • Tape the ends in the middle to secure them.
    • Attach a string to hang it up.
    • You can add decorations or tassels at the bottom.
  3. How to Prepare for a Fiesta:
    • Clean the house.
    • Make a list of dishes to cook.
    • Go to the market for ingredients.
    • Cook the dishes like pancit, lumpia, and barbecue.
    • Prepare the plates and glasses for the guests.
  4. How to Order at a Fast-Food (e.g., Jollibee):
    • Go to the counter and wait for your turn.
    • Tell the cashier your order, such as, "One Chickenjoy with rice, please."
    • Wait on the side to get your order.
    • Get tissues and condiments from the station.
    • Find a table and eat your food.
  5. How to Ride a Bicycle:
    • Sit on the bicycle seat.
    • Put your hands on the handlebars.
    • Push the pedals slowly.
    • Keep your balance.
    • Use the brakes when you need to stop.
